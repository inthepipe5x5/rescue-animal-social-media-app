Why Rescue?

Here are three compelling reasons why you should consider adopting a rescue animal instead of "shopping" for a pet.

Animal rescue refers to the efforts made to find, help, and ultimately rescue animals in bad situations through fostering, rehab or adopting to a loving home. Animal rescue organizations help find lost pets, respond to natural disasters to help animals stranded or injured, and rescuing animals being used for illegal activities such as dog fighting, poaching. Anyone can be part of animal rescue but it's hard to know where to start. That's where Far Fetched comes in.

Overpopulation Crisis
Every year, millions of animals end up in shelters due to overpopulation, abandonment, or owner surrender. Sadly, many of these animals are euthanized due to a lack of space and resources.
By adopting a rescue animal, you are providing a loving home to a deserving companion and helping to alleviate the burden on overcrowded shelters. By adopting one of these animals, you are giving them a second chance at life and the opportunity to experience the love and care they deserve.

Affordable Adoption Fees
Adopting a rescue animal is often more cost-effective than purchasing from a breeder or pet store, with adoption fees covering initial veterinary expenses. Adoption fees are still many times cheaper than purchasing through a recognized breeder.
Supporting a Worthy Cause
Your adoption fee supports animal welfare organizations' efforts to rescue, rehabilitate, and rehome animals in need.
Discouraging Irresponsible Breeding
Adopting discourages puppy mills and unethical breeding practices that prioritize profit over animal welfare. A cheap listing from Craigs' List might be cheaper than paying adoption fees but they are often bred irresponsibly and come with expensive hidden veterinary fees down the line.

Variety of Breeds and Ages
Rescue shelters offer a diverse selection of animals, from purebred to mixed breeds, and from puppies to seniors, allowing you to find the perfect companion.
Breed Specific Rescues
Want a specific animal breed? There's a dedicated rescue organization for almost every recognized animal breed.
Pre-evaluated Temperaments
Many rescue organizations evaluate the temperaments of their animals, making it easier to find a compatible match and avoid potential behavioral issues.
